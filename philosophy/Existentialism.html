Existentialism

Primarily, the brach of philosophy that comprises existentialism is mostly of theories that define the nature of being a person. It attempts to define the existance of a person and the implications of existance upon free will.

Existence Precedes Essence

This phrase comes from Jean-Paul Sarte. To summarize, there is no such thing as human nature. Through this idea, it is not supposed that we are born with a set of values. Our existence is made by our simply existing and what we make of ourselfs while doing so.
